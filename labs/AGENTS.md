# AGENTS.md - Labs

Labs are playgrounds for methodological exploration.

Future labs may be novel, weird, LLM-heavy, multimodal, multi-agent, or experimental.

But lab exports must remain boring and protocol-valid.

Inside a lab, ask:

* What research question is this testing?
* What method is being tried?
* What benchmark pack is used?
* What artifacts are produced?
* What failed?
* What should another lab reuse or avoid?

Do not turn a lab method into shared architecture.

Do not create live LLM runs until the live LLM experiment admission checklist is satisfied.

If a lab direction changes, update or replace stale lab docs. Do not append conflicting current claims.

## Lab Lifecycle Outputs

A meaningful lab change usually produces or updates:

* `LAB_CARD.md`
* `RESEARCH_QUESTION.md`
* method description
* run or export records
* evaluation notes
* negative-result notes
* stale direction cleanup

If one of these is intentionally absent, explain why in the lab notes or final work summary.

## New Method Hardening

Before adding files for a new lab method, reuse existing protocol records and adapt existing lab planning structure where possible.

Add only the minimum new files needed for the method's distinct research shape.

If a new file is added, explain why an existing lab file, fixture, planning pattern, or export record could not own that role.
