# Usefulness Review 001: Operator Review Form

Status: awaiting operator review — the operator's answers are the ground truth for the portfolio's first downstream-usefulness measurement

Instructions: read `study_card_candidates.md` (5 minutes). For each card, answer the four questions below — gut answers are fine and more useful than considered diplomacy. Then answer the three overall questions. Your answers get recorded as the usefulness ground truth (a research note in this lab), the same way your strict-review verdicts anchor the calibration study.

This form measures research usefulness for *you* as a trading student — not trading validity, not profitability, not whether the extraction was well-grounded (already reviewed).

---

## Per-card questions (answer for Card 1, Card 2, Card 3)

1. **Would you keep this card?** (yes / no / only if reworked)
2. **Does it tell you anything you'd actually act on in study or review** — e.g., something you'd check on a chart this week? (yes / vaguely / no)
3. **What's missing that the source taught but the card lost?** (free text, one line is fine)
4. **Is the locator handle useful to you** — would you ever click through lines 45–86 to reread the original, or is the citation dead weight? (useful / dead weight / only for auditing)

## Overall questions

5. **Format verdict:** is "principle + conditions + limitations + source lines" the right shape for study material, or would you prefer something else (flashcard Q&A, checklist, annotated transcript, chart-annotated examples)?
6. **Volume verdict:** the pilot produced 3 cards from a 945-word excerpt. For a full course transcript (~15k words), would ~50 cards of this quality be a useful study asset or noise?
7. **The north-star question:** after reading these, are you closer to "this pipeline could produce something I'd pay for / use weekly," or "interesting but not the artifact I need"? What artifact *do* you need?
